Understanding Graph Neural Networks (GNNs):

Graph Neural Networks are a type of neural network designed specifically for data represented as graphs. This makes them ideal for scenarios where data points are interconnected, such as social networks, molecular structures, or even research networks in medical fields.

  • Data Representation: In GNNs, data is represented as nodes (vertices) and relationships (edges), with each node containing attributes that describe its characteristics.
  • Feature Learning: GNNs are capable of learning complex patterns in the data by aggregating information from a nodeâ€™s neighbors, effectively capturing both local and global structures within the graph.
